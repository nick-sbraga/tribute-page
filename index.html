Harambe

May 27, 1999 - May 28, 2016

Harambe was a western lowland gorilla who lived at the Cincinnati Zoo.

Facts about Harambe:

  • Harambe was born May 27, 1999 at Gladys Porter Zoo in Brownsville, Texas.
  • He was named after the 1988 song "Harambe (Working Together for Freedom)"" by Rita Marley, widow of Bob Marley.
  • Harambee is a Swahili term for communal labor.
  • He was transferred to the Cincinnati Zoo on September 18, 2014 to join a new social group and to learn about adult gorilla behavior.
  • On May 28, 2016 Harambe was shot and killed after a three year old boy fell into the moat at the Gorilla World habitat.
